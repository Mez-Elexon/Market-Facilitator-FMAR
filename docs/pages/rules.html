Rule Development Process

The rule development process follows a collaborative approach involving stakeholders from across the market ecosystem.

Development Stages

  1. Proposal: Initial rule proposals from stakeholders
  2. Consultation: Public consultation and feedback collection
  3. Review: Expert review and impact assessment
  4. Approval: Regulatory approval and adoption
  5. Implementation: Rollout and enforcement

Stakeholder Engagement

Effective rule development requires input from diverse stakeholders:

  • Market Participants: Direct users of the market framework
  • Regulators: Oversight and enforcement authorities
  • Technology Providers: System and service providers
  • End Users: Consumers and energy users
  • Experts: Technical and legal specialists

Rule Categories

Market rules are organized into several key categories:

Operational Rules

Rules governing day-to-day market operations, trading procedures, and participant interactions.

Technical Rules

Technical specifications, interface requirements, and system integration standards.

Regulatory Rules

Compliance requirements, reporting obligations, and regulatory oversight procedures.

Governance Rules

Decision-making processes, stakeholder representation, and institutional arrangements.

Market Framework

Collaborative development of market frameworks and regulatory rules.
